WHY ICAF
Creativity and arts deliver the empathy needed for diversity and differences to expand the fractal of human discovery and evolution.
Why Creativity?
"Creativity is seeing what others see and thinking what no one else ever thought."
—Albert Einstein
Creativity sparks inventions, shifts paradigms, and brings disruptive innovations. Creativity is and has always been a survival skill. For those underprivileged, creativity boosts their confidence to blaze new trails that can disrupt the vicious cycle of intergenerational poverty. Unfortunately, human creative development faces a roadblock—the "4th-grade slump" in children's creativity discovered by E. Paul Torrance, the father of modern creativity. This slump grows into a crisis when standardized testing promotes rote learning and when students engage less in deep learning or seek experiences that inspire creativity because a plethora of information and entertainment is readily available online.

ICAF's school art program (the Arts Olympiad) empowers students to overcome the "4th-grade slump." The Arts Olympiad's structured lesson plans inspire students to embrace the Artist-Athlete Ideal of the creative mind and healthy body (mens sana in corpore sano). This paradigm awakens the dormant "inner artist" or the slumbering "inner athlete," liberating the natural tendency toward creativity and good health. Students realize that mind and body reflect each other because one's state of mind can have an epigenetic effect. The revised self-image solidifies when students depict themselves as artist-athletes in their artwork. As artist-athletes grow in number, the school transforms into a more integrated, creative, healthy community.
Why Empathy?
"Through art alone are we able to emerge from ourselves to know what another person sees."
—Marcel Proust
The word "empathy" is derived from einfühlung (literally "feeling into" something), which can describe what a beholder feels when facing a painting. Empathy is a vicarious, embodied identification and conscious involvement with another and has profound implications when the other is not an artist but is the enemy. Empathy is and has been Homo sapiens' survival skill. It fosters conflict resolution and stimulates collaborative innovation. It makes democracy workable and capitalism socially responsible. When empathy is absent, knowledge cannot evolve into wisdom. And awareness does not translate into action for social justice and poverty eradication or against climate change, the next big war, and pandemics.

Of the nearly 55 million children enrolled in PK 12 schools in the U.S., about 10% attend private schools, and nearly 80% of them religious schools. According to the National Center for Education Statistics, enrollment in conservative Christian, Jewish, and Islamic schools is increasing. Religious education can develop fellow feelings or "parochial" empathy among co-religionists. The late University of Pennsylvania neuroscientist Emile Bruneau found that the stronger the parochial empathy, the deeper the fear of or the hatred for outsiders and outgroups.

Public and private school students and those attending religious schools or are homeschooled come together at the World Children's Festival (WCF) in Washington, DC, every four years. ICAF's intervention at the WCF allows these young participants to co-imagine and co-create so they build trust and develop mutual empathy. The WCF's transformative experience boosts their self- confidence and self-worth as creators of the future so they can overcome adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) that might turn victims into perpetrators. Empathy training and social- emotional learning empower children to disrupt the transgenerational transmission of trauma, hatred, and retribution—so they fight not for their tribe's history but for "a more perfect union." By infusing creativity with empathy, the WCF inspires children to become creative-empaths.
Why Creatve-Empaths?
"Hope for humanity rises from creativity (that can be learned) and empathy (that can be taught)."
—Ashfaq Ishaq
Creativity is a salient human attribute, but it is morally neutral. Malevolent creativity abounds with intentionality to harm other people or society as a whole. What makes creativity positive to advance civilization and humanity? Empathy. In reality, creativity and empathy are like two hands that complement and enrich each other. Creativity clears the pathway to reach its pinnacle, which is empathy. Empathy imbues creativity with the diversity of human experiences for global competency and universality. A creative mind and an empathic heart make a person wholesome. Only creative-empaths can create a more peaceful, sustainable, and prosperous future.
Why the Arts?
"The task of art is enormous."
—Leo Tolstoy
The 2021 report Art for Life's Sake: The Case for Arts Education of the American Academy of Arts & Sciences provides "evidence of the attributes, values, and skills that come from arts education, including social and emotional development, improvements in school engagement, as well as vital civic and social participation." Art programs are effective and also the least-cost interventions.

ICAF employs the arts as a dynamic activity for students' creative and empathic development. Art grows a child's imagination, and collaborative mural-making can become a building block for self- discovery and collaborative innovation. The arts can change a person. ICAF leverages the power of the arts for students to enhance their self-identity, first as artist-athletes through the Arts Olympiad, then as creative-empaths at the WCF.
Why Take Action?
"If you want real peace in the world, start with children."
—Mahatma Gandhi
With school programs that are free of charge, educational festivals that are free and open to the public, and ChildArt magazine that is free of commercial advertisements, ICAF relies on creative- empaths that adopt ICAF as their charity of choice. You, too, can be a creative-empath! Children deserve your attention and support.
