                                    Creativity and color collided  in the workshops provided  by 
a team of artists from Nigeria.  Traveling  more than 5,000 
miles, the Nigerian delegation brought their traditions of rich 
and vibrant art to Washington DC, educating students in art 
techniques as well as the far-reaching positive outcomes of 
art experiences.
The delegation was arranged for by Lady Ngozi Akande, an 
ICAF representative and board member. Ngozi is co-founder of 
the Female Artists Association of Nigeria and uses her art and 
experience to advocate for women and children and mentor 
young artists. Just prior to the WCF she was promoted to head 
of the Department of Arts and Crafts at the National Council 
for Arts and Culture, Abuja, Nigeria.
The Nigerian  delegation  was made up of four members 
of the Female  Artists  Association of Nigeria  (FEAAN): 
Professor Bridget Nwanze,  (BOT Chairman),  Mrs. Chinze 
Ojobo (President), Millicent Okocha (Treasurer), and Dr. Rita 
Doris Ubah (Southeast Coordinator). They spoke about the 
importance of art, emphasizing that parents should encourage 
their children to develop their artistic skills, and also embrace 
art as a form of therapy. They highlighted the impact of art on 
a child’s development. “Through art, one can see and read the 
mind of a child just as through sport. Art and sport can also 
be used as cultural exchange for peaceful co-existence.” The 
team explored the pedagogical methods to facilitate, instruct, 
process, and actualize the festival’s themes of empathy and 
peace in their workshop activities
